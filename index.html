Watchtower: Orbital.Strike\Fleet.Command (Starfield Creations)
by: kinggath_creations
Features

A new faction has emerged from the shadows to protect humanity from potential threats with excessive power - and they've identified YOU as their latest target.

  • New Storyline - Join forces with a fellow adventurer hunted by Watchtower, befriend their contacts, and work together to pilfer Watchtower’s technology and turn it against them - as Watchtower has done to the threats they hunt. For Unity-jumpers, the story is designed with extensive additional dialog, options, and reactivity on subsequent experiences.
  • Build a Fleet - Crew all of your ships and have them fight beside you in space, provide planetary support with orbital strikes, and help protect you from the growing threat that is Watchtower.
  • Strike from Above - Unlock powerful new ship attachments known as Skydrops which allow your fleet to provide support drops or rain down attacks from orbit to help you as you adventure planet-side.
  • Purge the Galaxy - Work to uncover and dismantle Watchtower's network of listening posts and spy satellite arrays, then take on their impressive fleet of ships with your own so you and your allies can live without fear of ambush.
  • A Home in Space - Watch as a derelict starstation evolves and becomes a hub to you, your crew, and spacefarers from around the galaxy.
  • Exotic Equipment - New equipment can be taken from Watchtower troops for you and your squad, or unlocked for production - such as the new facial attachments known as Cyberamps which can augment the piloting skills of you and your crew.

As you engage more with the mechanics and story – additional depth, options, and rewards will be revealed. Watchtower is designed to work seamlessly with the base game, DLCs, and many other community mods.

How to use this Creation

Whether a new character, an existing character, or a unity jump, the Creation will start the same way. Hop in your ship and travel between systems level 15 or higher, and you'll eventually be confronted by agents of Watchtower who will hail you. The various systems will be introduced to you as you play through the questline. Around the midpoint of the questline, you'll be set free to engage at your own pace and pursue the systems you enjoy the most.

Cryomancers - A New Faction
By: zone79

A new faction introduced to the game that stemmed from the Spacers. They are more organized, have their own facilities, and use Cryogenics in all their science/technology. They aim to slow, freeze, and break anyone that stands in their way as they go unchecked in their advancements.

ENEMY TYPES
  • Human = Scientist, Lab Worker, Miner, Robotics, Scout, Sniper, Assault, Charger, Heavy, and Boss
  • Altered Human = Cryo Brute
  • Creatures = Trained, Wild, Alpha, and Altered Cryobeasts
  • Robots = ModelA Cryobot, ModelS Cryobot, CryoDrone
  • Turrets = TallTurret, ShortTurret, Anti-Ship Cannons, Tanks
  • Dropships that release more enemies at your location
WEAPONS & GEAR
  • 12 Weapons
  • 4 Spacesuits (5 if you count a retextured mining spacesuit)
  • 8 Backpacks
  • 18 Helmets
  • 1 Bandolier (worn with spacesuit)
  • 10 Clothing items (outfits, masks, goggles, hats, backpacks)
WHAT THIS MOD DOES

When you walk around in The Well you'll get a mission that will lead you through the Cryomancer's 4 main headquarters. Along the way you will come across 3 companions with the option to join you. You will also find a new ship with a custom interior that is themed around this new faction. Those with the skill, and a good eye will come across 5 very strong unique weapons. Lastly there is a player home, or "Lair" you can get at the end of it all. It comes fully furnished with everything you could need, and is full of buttons/switches to interact with. Mission aside there are nearly 30 new randomly generated POIs you can discover. As you explore the many planets you'll come across them ranging from crash sights, caves, facilities, to large dungeons (The names will stand out, making them easy to spot). This new faction scales in level with the player, and has had their stats slightly boosted more than the others to making it so every encounter can be challenging. They also have their own locations, loot table, and items, making it compatible with all other creations.

Keep Kryx's Legacy for yourself, or give some or all of it to the Freestar Collective, or House Va'ruun.

Main Features

  • Be warned, certain choices will lock you out of most of the epilogue

COMPATABILITY

If you intend to use this and Dark Universe - Crossfire, you will reach a certain point in the quest where you have to grav jump to a space station. Make a save immediately before grav jumping. Upon jumping in, you should see the station, 5 friendly ships close to the station, and some icy asteroids. If you see anything on top of this (such as hostile or neutral ships, or any space debris other than ice asteroids), then DU - Crossfire has injected a space encounter into the cell, and I highly recommend reloading the save you made before grav jumping, to prevent the space encounter from interfering with the events that are about to unfold.

Useful Brigs - almost all of the enemies you fight from the time you select [Commit] in dialogue, to the point where you get back to your ship, will be disabled at a later time. If you took any of these enemies prisoner, I recommend either releasing them from your brig, or turning them in before you progress the quest to the point where you travel to a certain space station.

If you still have some of these enemies in your brig at a certain point after boarding the space station, they will be disabled, and potentially break your brig. Any enemies you face after traveling to the space station can be safely taken prisoner without limitation. However, certain enemies are immune to EM damage, and immune to social skills.

Q&A:

Is having played The Veil required?
No, but it is highly recommended. If you haven't played The Veil, you will receive a short summary during your first dialogue in this quest.

If I have both The Veil and Beyond the Veil installed, do I need to complete The Veil first?
There are no checks to see if you have completed The Veil, but Beyond the Veil takes place after its events. For immersion, complete The Veil first.

Dark Universe - Takeover (Nexus Mods)
By: kaosnyrb

Adds 50 new procedurally generated Spacer Military Bases, supporting bounties.

How does it work?

The bases are made up of parts which are combined by a program I've written called Startiller. These are then output to a format that Starfield understands. The POI's are exactly like the vanilla ones, there is no scripting or dynamic changes that happen. They use the same enemy references as vanilla so any mods affecting spacers will also work in the new POI's. This makes them very compatible with other mods.

How to find them?

Bounty board missions will start offering missions to these POI's on the next refresh (Might need to rest for a while to get new ones).They've been tweaked so as not to be the only missions available but to show up reasonable often.
They also are visible from space during scanning and will start appearing in unexplored systems once you install this mod.
Finally you can just land anywhere and you've a chance of coming across one.

⚠️ Special Mod Notes & Conflict Warnings

Gangs All Here

Gangs All Here is a community-made mod that restores full companion functionality so you can play Starfield the way it feels it should have been built. Because it works around the game’s core systems rather than being integrated into them, there are some trade-offs.

  • Main story quests: Use only one companion while running main questlines to avoid breaking quest scripts.
  • Companion-specific storylines: New bindable companions and companions’ personal questlines can conflict with the mod; expect possible quest failures or progression issues. Either disable the mod or bring only 1 companion. It does natively support locking companions and not allowing additional companions until the quest is complete (in mod settings).
  • Other story quests: Some story-driven quests may also be affected; save often before starting any quest.
  • General play and combat: For side quests and combat, you can add companions more freely, but stability varies.
  • Practical recommendation: For the smoothest experience, limit active companions to two, three at most.

Upgraded Companions

Load Upgraded Companions BEFORE The Gang's All Here in your load order.

Legacy's End - Wild Card

  • Using with The Gangs All Here: Like many narratives, ONLY bring 1 companion or it may break!
  • Interaction with Dark Universe - Crossfire: This quest adds a new space cell where space encounters are not allowed to happen. Due to the way DU - Crossfire works, it bypasses this restriction and can inject its own space encounters into the cell. This can potentially interfere with the quest's progression, and one user has reported that it may have been the cause of a repeatable game crash.
  • If you intend to use this and Dark Universe - Crossfire at the same time: You will reach a certain point in the quest where you have to grav jump to a space station. Make a save immediately before grav jumping. Upon jumping in, you should see the station, 5 friendly ships close to the station, and some icy asteroids. If you see anything on top of this (such as hostile or neutral ships, or any space debris other than ice asteroids), then DU - Crossfire has injected a space encounter into the cell, and it is highly recommended to reload the save made before grav jumping, to prevent the space encounter from interfering with the events that are about to unfold.
  • Fleet Mods: Space combat is balanced around the player having only one ship. If you use a mod like Fleet Commander or Watchtower to bring an entire fleet of friendly ships, you might find space combat to be very easy.
  • Interaction with Useful Brigs: Under no circumstances should you try to subdue a friendly, named quest NPC and send them to your brig. If they are already hostile to you, then fair game. As far as taking unnamed NPCs prisoner: this quest attempts to clean up after itself by disabling certain enemy bodies after the quest progresses past a certain point. If you have taken some of these enemies prisoner and they are in your brig when they get disabled, they will potentially break your brig, causing you to be unable to turn them in, or add new prisoners.

Chunks Noir

Known Issue: Due to a change to the Athena Tower Elevators, the player will need to do a cell/location reset on New Atlantis IF they are starting the mod with a pre-established save/character (If the player is starting a fresh new game or entering New Game + after downloading this mod, this issue will not occur). The quickest way to do this is to start in New Atlantis, then fly to Venus and wait for about 10 in-game hours. This should properly adjust the elevator for the rest of the player's playthrough (This will also work if this issue ever arises again or appears with the base game quest "Alternating Currents").

The Silent Covenant

Silent Covenant, Falkland Systems with New Atlantis Awaits Mod conflict: Both modify overlapping exterior and interior worldspace cells in New Atlantis. Because each mod places a custom facility, entrance markers, persistent references, and navmesh edits in the same physical coordinates, they directly overwrite one another. Disable the Falkland mod until you complete the Silent Covenant Quests. Once complete activate the Falkland mod. Alternatively: Disable the NAA - Falkland Patch (though the elevator remains broken and you will have to jump down into the waterfall to get to the door). Once finished reenable the NAA - Patch mod, otherwise you will run into an invisible wall when entering Falkland!

Conflict Types: Worldspace cell overlap, Exterior cell overwrite, Interior cell overwrite, Persistent reference conflict, Navmesh conflict, Door teleport marker conflict, POI trigger conflict.

Stroud Premium Edition & Place Doors Yourself

On the mod page for Stroud Premium Edition, the author explicitly states that Place Doors Yourself (PDY) is a soft requirement: "Place Doors Yourself is not required but HIGHLY recommended if you want to enjoy all the features of each hab and avoid strange door placement."
