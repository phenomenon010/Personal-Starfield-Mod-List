### Special Note About Conflicts or Potential Issues

##### [**Gangs All Here**](https://www.nexusmods.com/starfield/mods/7469)

Gangs All Here is a community-made mod that restores full companion functionality so you can play Starfield the way it feels it should have been built. Because it works around the game’s core systems rather than being integrated into them, there are some trade-offs.



* **Main story quests**: Use only one companion while running main questlines to avoid breaking quest scripts.
* **Companion-specific storylines:** New bindable companions and companions’ personal questlines can conflict with the mod; expect possible quest failures or progression issues. either disable the mod or bring only 1 companion, It does natively support locking companions and not allowing additional companions till the quest is complete (in mod settings).
* **Other story quests:** Some story-driven quests may also be affected; save often before starting any quest.
* **General play and combat:** For side quests and combat, you can add companions more freely, but stability varies.
* **Practical recommendation:** For the smoothest experience, limit active companions to two, three at most.



##### [**Upgraded Companions**](https://www.nexusmods.com/starfield/mods/12030)

* Load Upgraded Companions BEFORE The Gang's All Here.



##### [**Legacy's End - Wild Card**](https://www.nexusmods.com/starfield/mods/14225)

* Using with ***The Gangs All Here*** Like many narratives, **ONLY bring 1 companion** or it may break!



* **Interaction with *Dark Universe - Crossfire﻿*** - My quest adds a new space cell where space encounters are not allowed to happen. Due to the way DU - Crossfire works, it bypasses this restriction, and can inject its own space encounters in to the cell. This can potentially interfere with my quest's progression, and one user has reported that it may have been the cause of a repeatable game crash.



* **If you intend to use this and *Dark Universe - Crossfire*** at the same time - you will reach a certain point in my quest where you have to grav jump to a space station. Make a save immediately before grav jumping. Upon jumping in, you should see the station, 5 friendly ships close to the station, and some icy asteroids. If you see anything on top of this (such as hostile or neutral ships, or any space debris other than ice asteroids), then DU - Crossfire has injected a space encounter into the cell, and I highly recommend reloading the save you made before grav jumping, to prevent the space encounter from interfering with the events that are about to unfold.



* Space combat is balanced around the player having only one ship. If you use a mod like ***Fleet Commander or Watchtower*** to bring an entire fleet of friendly ships, you might find space combat to be very easy.



* ***Interaction with Useful Brigs﻿*** - Under no circumstances should you try to subdue a friendly, named quest NPC and send them to your brig. If they are already hostile to you, then fair game. As far as taking unnamed NPCs prisoner: my quest attempts to clean up after itself by disabling certain enemy bodies after the quest progresses past a certain point. If you have taken some of these enemies prisoner and they are in your brig when they get disabled, they will potentially break your brig, causing you to be unable to turn them in, or add new prisoners. If you want exact details on which enemies you can and can't take prisoner, see the spoilers section at the bottom.



##### [**Chunks Noir**](https://www.nexusmods.com/starfield/mods/12749)

KNOWN ISSUE:  Due to a change to the Athena Tower Elevators, the player will need to do a cell/location reset on New Atlantis IF they are starting the mod with a pre-established save/character (If the player is starting a fresh new game or entering New Game + after downloading this mod, this issue will not occur). The quickest way to do this is to start in New Atlantis, then fly to Venus and wait for about 10 in-game hours. This should properly adjust the elevator for the rest of the player's playthrough (This will also work if this issue ever arises again or appears with the base game quest "Alternating Currents").



##### [**The Silent Covenant**](https://creations.bethesda.net/en/starfield/details/6779e8b2-b464-44d1-9285-deebc452e6fb/The_Silent_Covenant)  (Starfield Creations)

**Silent Covenant, Falkland Systems with New Atlantis Awaits Mod** both modify overlapping exterior and interior worldspace cells in new Atlantis. Because each mod places a custom facility, entrance markers, persistent references, and navmesh edits in the same physical coordinates, they directly overwrite one another. Disable the Falkland mod until you complete the Silent Covenant Quests. Once complete Activate Falkland mod. **ALTERNATIVLY:** I did have success with just disabling the NAA - Falkland Patch but the elevator remained broken I had to jump down into the waterfall to get to the door. Once finished reenable the NAA - Patch mod, otherwise you will run into an invisible wall when entering Falkland!

**Conflict Type:**

* Worldspace cell overlap
* Exterior cell overwrite
* Interior cell overwrite
* Persistent reference conflict
* Navmesh conflict
* Door teleport marker conflict
* POI trigger conflict



##### **Stroud Premium Edition**

On the mod page for Stroud Premium Edition, the author explicitly states that Place Doors Yourself (PDY) is a soft requirement: "Place Doors Yourself is not required but HIGHLY recommended if you want to enjoy all the features of each hab and avoid strange door placement."
